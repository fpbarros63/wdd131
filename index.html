Recife, Brazil

Recife is the "Brazilian Venice" because of its rivers and bridges, it has the largest carnival parade in the world (Galo da Madrugada), the first synagogue in the Americas, it is home to baobab trees outside of Africa and houses Porto Digital, one of the largest technology parks in Brazil, in addition to a rich culture with Frevo, Manguebeat, and delicacies such as bolo de rolo (rolled cake), also being a historically old and densely populated city, known for its gastronomy and cultural diversity.
